Project Manager - Performance, Accountability & Service Framework

Conductor of the Orchestra: Creating Harmony Between Expectations and Execution
Customer Delight & Team Development
The Conductor
Overarching Goal: To be the conductor of the orchestra, transforming flawless operational execution into stakeholder delight and team growth. You are accountable for the human systemaligning customer needs, team capability, and business outcomes to create predictable, high-quality, and growing delivery.
Growth Killer to Avoid: Shying away from difficult conversationswhether with underperforming team members or unhappy clientsas this erodes trust and prevents necessary growth and change.

Category 1: Customer & Stakeholder Orchestration (The Trust Architect)

Goal: To own the customer relationship, manage expectations proactively, and build trust that transforms delivery from a transactional service into a strategic partnership. You are accountable for the client's perception of value and the health of the commercial relationship.

Category 2: Team Leadership & Talent Development (The People Catalyst)

Goal: To own the health, growth, and high performance of the delivery team. You are accountable for creating an environment where individuals thrive, skills grow, and the team reliably exceeds expectations. You shift from managing tasks to cultivating capability.

Team Enablement & Performance Governance
ACCOUNTABLE FOR OBJECTIVE(S) KEY KPIS & METRICS REQUIRED COMPETENCIES SLAS / ORGANIZATIONAL NORMS
  • Develop junior team members while managing delivery
  • Enable team members by removing roadblocks
  • Mentor team members on technical and soft skills
  • Monitor and govern team performance
  • Address and resolve team conflicts constructively
  • Monitor workload to prevent burnout
  • Ensure an even distribution of workload across team members to maintain productivity
  • Monitor task progress and reassign resources where necessary to meet deadlines
  • Encourage celebrating wins and acknowledge contributions
  • Manage REO (Reflect, Connect, Reorient) sessions with direct reports as part of the Immediate Reporting Person (IRP) responsibility
  • Maintain =95% team retention rate
  • Achieve =80% of team members reporting high growth and job satisfaction in surveys
  • Keep average team workload =40 hours/week (monitored via time logs)
  • Facilitate =4 team development activities per quarter (workshops, knowledge shares)
  • Have =1 team conflict requiring formal HR mediation per year
  • Ensure =4 REO sessions per direct report per quarter with documented outcomes
  • Voluntary Attrition Rate
  • Team Growth & Satisfaction Index (Internal survey)
  • Average Weekly Hours per Team Member (with variance analysis)

Coaching & Mentoring Emotional Intelligence (EQ) Delegation Performance Management Motivational Leadership Structured Feedback Delivery

Goal: To own the financial integrity and commercial hygiene of the projects under your purview. You are accountable for ensuring delivery translates into accurate revenue recognition, healthy margins, and data that feeds business decisions. You connect team effort to company P&L.

Evolution Lens (The Conductor)

The Project Manager framework is about mastering influence and systems thinking. You are the translator between the operational engine and the strategic business.

From "Task Manager" to "Team Architect"

You evolve from overseeing tasks to designing the team systemits roles, growth paths, workload balance, and culturethat sustainably produces results.

From "Reporter" to "Storyteller & Negotiator"

You shift from sending status updates to crafting narratives that manage stakeholder perceptions, justify investments, and negotiate through delivery risks and scope changes.

From "Process Follower" to "Process Innovator"

You move from enforcing existing processes to critically evaluating and redesigning them for greater efficiency, quality, and team satisfaction.

From "Cost Center Manager" to "Commercial Steward"

Your perspective expands from managing project budgets to understanding how your delivery impacts the broader P&L, client lifetime value, and invoice-to-cash cycles.

Your Success Is Measured By...

Relationship metrics (CSAT, escalations), people metrics (retention, satisfaction, growth), and commercial metrics (forecast accuracy, invoicing hygiene, process improvement). You build the trust that allows the business to grow.

Critical Performance Insight

Project Delivery Managers must dedicate at least 60% of their time to strategic activities (relationship building, talent development, process innovation). Anything less indicates disproportionate focus on operational details rather than higher-value leadership responsibilities.
